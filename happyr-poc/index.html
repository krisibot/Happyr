How it works

One platform, three local engines

🚲

Last-mile delivery

Restaurants, grocers, pharmacies, and independent merchants reach nearby customers using e-bike riders, cutting costs and emissions while keeping margins local.

🛏️

Community stays

An Airbnb-style layer for hosts who want fairer fees, transparent reviews, and an ownership stake in the marketplace itself.

🔗

Programmable payouts

Smart contracts split proceeds automatically between workers, vendors, hosts, and a shared co-op treasury with auditable rules.

Co-op economics

The money flow is the product

Instead of extraction

  • Vendors keep the majority of order value
  • Riders and hosts receive transparent, immediate earnings
  • A small network fee funds shared operations and member benefits
  • Members vote on treasury use, fee policy, and expansion

Cape Town pilot

Start where density, culture, and mobility already exist

Why Cape Town first?

Dense neighbourhoods, existing cycling culture, strong tourism flows, and a visible appetite for alternatives to extractive platforms.

Pilot zones could include Sea Point, Green Point, CBD, Gardens, Woodstock, and Muizenberg-style stay clusters.

What the pilot proves

  • Lower-cost local delivery with e-bikes
  • Better take-home earnings for workers
  • Direct merchant adoption
  • Trust in blockchain-backed payout visibility
  • A replicable co-op model for South African cities
Why now

South Africa does not need another middleman app.

It needs infrastructure that shares upside with the people creating the value. Happyr is a marketplace, yes — but more importantly, it is a mechanism for fair distribution.
